NaPoWriMo Day 24

Pain is the Way Our Bodies Tell Us No

A hand on a stove.
A slip on a bit of ice in the too cold December.
A paper cut.
A blister.
That day you got too drunk and told your friend he needs to "man up."
That day someone told you, you look like a slut.
When you pick up the glass shards with bare hands.
When you drink your hot coffee.
When you think about the Hot Coffee McDonalds documentary.
Every time you ever eat McDonalds.
Any time you see someone struggling.
A nightmare that swallows you.
A heart twist loss.
When you've been alone too long.
When you've always been together, but you grow apart.
When the TV tells you who you should be.
Every time anyone tells you who you should be.
That day your friend called you and said her dog got ran over by a car.
That day you walked home and told your friend you were angry that she didn't come get you.
A feeling of being forgotten.
A sad song welling up inside you like oil out of the borehole.
The closer you get to death.
When you bring someone into this world.
